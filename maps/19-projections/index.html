Ideation: 6 Concepts for GIS Day

Asked Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini 3 for projection visualizations that would be "meaningful, not just cool." Got 6 solid ideas spanning morphing animations, distortion comparisons, and educational dashboards.

Chosen Gemini 3
Shape Shifter

Animated morphing between unusual projections (Equirectangular → Mollweide → Berghaus Star → Sinusoidal). Graticule bends with landmasses to show transformation process.

Why chosen: Morphing IS the math—visualizes actual transformation, not decoration.

Chosen Gemini 3
Tissot's Lie Detector

Interactive Mercator vs Equal Area comparison with Tissot's Indicatrix circles revealing distortion. Morph slider + side-by-side modes.

Why chosen: Makes invisible distortion visible—complements Shape Shifter's transformation focus.

Claude
Projection Metamorphosis

Animated morphing between 6+ unusual projections (Waterman Butterfly, Dymaxion, Peirce Quincuncial) with smooth D3 interpolation.

ChatGPT
Unfolded Earth

Butterfly atlas where the world unfolds/refolds between Equal Earth and Waterman-style projection. Continents bend and hinge like origami.

Claude
Distortion Dashboard

2×2 grid comparing 4 projections (Mercator, Equal Earth, Azimuthal Equidistant, Winkel Tripel) with synchronized Tissot's indicatrices.

ChatGPT
Mercator's Big Lie

Split-screen comparison between Mercator and Equal Earth with animated Tissot circles that "breathe" from distorted ellipses to proper circles.

Why Dual Concepts?

Instead of testing 8-12 platforms with one concept (the usual Day 1-6 approach), Day 19 tested whether Gemini 3 could handle two complementary challenges: complex animations (Shape Shifter) and educational precision (Tissot). This validated Gemini 3's versatility more than spreading thin across weaker platforms.

Implementation 1: Shape Shifter

Gemini 3 Pro – Google AI Studio

Grade: A+ ✓ Deployed ✓ Mobile

Status: Working in 3 prompts, polished in ~8 more

URL: shape-shifter-gis-day-903076358991.us-west1.run.app

What Works:

  • Smooth morphing between 6+ projections (Equirectangular, Mollweide, Berghaus Star, Sinusoidal)
  • Graticule deforms along with landmasses
  • Animation controls: Play/Pause, Reset, Skip, Speed (0.5x-5x)
  • Real-time projection labels with educational descriptions
  • Dark theme with cyan accents

Technical Stack:

  • D3.js (d3-geo, d3-geo-projection) for projection interpolation
  • Natural Earth 110m land data from world-atlas CDN
  • SVG rendering with smooth transitions
  • Mobile-responsive layout

Note: Post-deployment mobile refinements included better touch targets and improved projection info card layouts.

Key Insights

Gemini 3 Continues Strong Performance

After Day 18's platform discovery, Day 19 confirms Gemini 3 Pro in Google AI Studio works well for map challenges:

  • Fast initial results: Both concepts working in 3 prompts
  • Iterative polish: Each follow-up prompt added useful improvements
  • Technical accuracy: Projection math, D3 usage, and animation interpolation all correct
  • Feature expansion: Easy to add projections, view modes, and controls through iteration
Meaningful Animation Achieved

The requirement for "meaningful animation" (not just decoration) was met:

  • Shape Shifter: Morphing IS the math—visualizes actual transformation process
  • Tissot: "Breathing circles" reveal distortion during projection transitions
  • Both make invisible mathematics visible
Strategy Shift Works Well

Day 19 validates dual-concept approach:

  • Testing 2 concepts with 1 strong platform vs 1 concept across 8-12 platforms
  • Proves versatility: complex animations + educational precision
  • Time efficient: ~30 mins total vs Day 17's 12+ hours
  • Higher success rate: 2/2 Grade A+ vs mixed results from multi-platform testing
Post-Deployment Iteration Valuable

Testing on actual devices revealed mobile UX issues:

  • Shape Shifter: Needed better touch targets and info card layouts
  • Tissot: Added collapsible settings panel for cleaner mobile interface
  • Gemini 3 implemented fixes quickly (2-3 prompts per app)
  • Preview-to-production gap remains (works in preview, verify in production)
D3 Projection Handling

Technical notes on what worked well:

  • Correct use of d3.geoProjection() and projection interpolation
  • Smooth transitions between projection states
  • CDN-hosted world-atlas data loaded without issues
  • SVG rendering performed well on mobile (no dropped frames)

Technical Details

Shape Shifter Tech Stack
  • D3.js (v7) with d3-geo and d3-geo-projection
  • TopoJSON client for data parsing
  • Natural Earth 110m land data (CDN)
  • SVG rendering
  • Projection interpolation for morphing
  • requestAnimationFrame for animation loop
Tissot Tech Stack
  • D3.js (v7) with extended d3-geo-projection
  • TopoJSON client for data parsing
  • Natural Earth 110m countries data (CDN)
  • SVG rendering (dual views)
  • d3.geoCircle() for Tissot circles
  • Slider interpolation for morph mode
Data Sources
