Why This Detour Matters

What Changed

When I learned that Gemini 3 Pro was available in Google's AI Studio, I had to test it. The platform that struggled on Days 1-6 with blank pages and rendering failures suddenly created Grade A maps in 2-3 prompts. This wasn't an incremental improvement‚Äîthis was a platform transformation.

What Changed
  • One-shot quality: First generation was already solid, just 2-3 prompts to Grade A
  • Real data integration: USGS earthquake API and OpenFlights database loaded seamlessly
  • Timeline animations: Easy to add smooth playback controls and time-based visualizations
  • Mobile responsiveness: All apps designed mobile-first by default
  • Visual polish: Dark themes, modern UI, thoughtful design
The Testing Approach

Instead of the standard multi-platform workflow, I tested Gemini 3 by recreating/reimagining maps from previous days. This allowed direct comparison with earlier platform results and validated whether Gemini 3 deserved Tier 1 status.

Editorial

A Platform Arrives

Day 18 was supposed to be "Out of this world" ‚Äì exoplanets, fantasy maps, Mars colonies. Instead, it became something more important: the moment Google AI Studio went from footnote to frontrunner.

When you're testing 8-12 platforms daily, you develop a sense for incremental progress. Gemini 2.5 in AI Studio was consistently Grade D or F on Days 1-6. Blank pages, rendering failures, basic functionality broken. Then Gemini 3 Pro arrived, and the platform transformed overnight.

SeismicWatch was the proof: Grade A on essentially the first try, with timeline animations and earthquake shaking effects added in just 2-3 prompts total. It matched or beat the best implementations from ChatGPT Canvas, Bolt.new, and Lovable ‚Äì platforms I'd spent the challenge praising. The Climate Migration choropleth with its smooth timeline slider reached Grade B+ just as quickly.

All 4 apps successfully deployed and working in production. Implementations ranged from Grade A (SeismicWatch) to Grade B- (Cosmic Sightings), showing strong performance across the board with varying data quality and complexity. The platform performs well across all implementations.

This detour matters because it reframes the remaining days. Gemini 3 isn't just another platform to test ‚Äì it's now a Tier 1 contender. The standard workflow going forward includes Gemini 3 alongside the proven performers. And that's worth documenting, even if it means postponing Mars colonies to another day.

The 30-day challenge is partly about parallel platform testing, partly about real-world documentation of what works and what doesn't in November 2025. Some discoveries are worth breaking the pattern for. This was one of them.
