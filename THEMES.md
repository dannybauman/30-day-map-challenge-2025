# All 30 Themes - 30 Day Map Challenge 2025

Complete list of themes with categories, descriptions, and personal notes.

---

## Week 1: Foundation & Classics

### Day 1 (Nov 1): Points
**Category**: Challenge Classic
**Description**: Point data (locations, POIs, clusters). Focus on symbolization and density.

**Ideas**:
- Coffee shops density map
- Star map / astronomical data
- WiFi hotspots
- Historic landmarks clustering
- Bird sighting locations

**Data Sources**: OpenStreetMap, personal GPS data, public APIs

---

### Day 2 (Nov 2): Lines
**Category**: Challenge Classic
**Description**: Linear features (roads, rivers, flows). Explore thickness, color, direction.

**Ideas**:
- River systems with flow direction
- Flight paths / shipping routes
- Hiking trails with elevation
- Public transit lines
- Migration patterns

**Data Sources**: Natural Earth, OpenStreetMap, USGS

---

### Day 3 (Nov 3): Polygons
**Category**: Challenge Classic
**Description**: Area features (regions, land use, boundaries). Use fills, patterns, choropleths.

**Ideas**:
- Land use / zoning map
- Climate zones
- Population density choropleth
- Protected areas / national parks
- Geological formations

**Data Sources**: Census data, Natural Earth, protected planet

---

### Day 4 (Nov 4): My Data
**Category**: Data Challenge
**Description**: Map something personal using your own dataset (GPS traces, commute, unique data).

**Ideas**:
- Personal location history
- Running/cycling routes
- Places visited this year
- Coffee shops I've been to
- Photo locations from phone

**Data Sources**: Google Takeout, Strava, personal logs

---

### Day 5 (Nov 5): Earth
**Category**: Classical Elements (1/4)
**Description**: Landforms, geology, soil, agriculture, elevation—anything solid beneath your feet.

**Ideas**:
- Topographic relief map
- Soil types and agriculture
- Geological age map
- Mountain ranges 3D
- Tectonic plate boundaries

**Data Sources**: USGS, SRTM elevation data, geological surveys

---

### Day 6 (Nov 6): Dimensions
**Category**: Creative
**Description**: Beyond 2D: 3D models, extrusions, depth, time as a dimension, multivariate.

**Ideas**:
- 3D city model with building heights
- Time-lapse of urban growth
- Multivariate symbol map
- Population pyramid as 3D landscape
- Elevation + time animation

**Data Sources**: OSM 3D, historical census, elevation data

---

### Day 7 (Nov 7): Accessibility
**Category**: Thematic
**Description**: Travel time, barriers, inclusive design, public transport reach, accessible maps.

**Ideas**:
- Isochrone map (15-min city)
- Wheelchair accessible routes
- Public transit coverage
- Distance to hospitals/schools
- Color-blind friendly design

**Data Sources**: Transit APIs, accessibility databases, OSM

---

### Day 8 (Nov 8): Urban
**Category**: Thematic (World Urbanism Day)
**Description**: Built environment: street networks, highrises, sprawl, city infrastructure, density.

**Ideas**:
- Street network analysis
- Building footprint density
- Urban heat island
- Green space distribution
- Growth comparison over time

**Data Sources**: OSM, satellite imagery, municipal data

---

## Week 2: Creativity & Speed

### Day 9 (Nov 9): Analog
**Category**: Creative
**Description**: Step away from screens! Pen, pencil, paint, collage, physical models.

**Ideas**:
- Hand-drawn neighborhood map
- Watercolor terrain
- Cut-paper relief map
- Embroidered cartography
- Clay 3D model (then photograph)

**Data Sources**: Your hands! Then digitize for documentation

---

### Day 10 (Nov 10): Air
**Category**: Classical Elements (2/4)
**Description**: Atmosphere: weather, wind, air traffic, pollution, airborne transmission.

**Ideas**:
- Wind patterns visualization
- Air quality index map
- Flight tracking over time
- Weather fronts animation
- Pollen count regions

**Data Sources**: Weather APIs, flight trackers, EPA data

---

### Day 11 (Nov 11): Minimal map
**Category**: Design Challenge
**Description**: Fewest elements possible while staying clear, useful, and informative.

**Ideas**:
- Subway map with 3 colors max
- Coastline only, no labels
- Single data point per country
- Monochrome elevation
- Just borders, no fills

**Data Sources**: Any, but simplify dramatically

---

### Day 12 (Nov 12): Map from 2125
**Category**: Speculative
**Description**: How will maps look 100 years from now? Create a future vision.

**Ideas**:
- Sea level rise impacts
- Mars colony map
- Neural interface cartography
- Climate-changed biomes
- Floating city networks

**Data Sources**: Climate projections, speculative design, imagination

---

### Day 13 (Nov 13): 10 minute map
**Category**: Speed Challenge
**Description**: Maximum 10 minutes to design and produce. Speed + simplicity.

**Ideas**:
- Quick OSM extraction
- Simple choropleth
- Point pattern
- Graticule art
- Fast sketch map

**Data Sources**: Pre-downloaded datasets, quick APIs

---

### Day 14 (Nov 14): OpenStreetMap
**Category**: Data Challenge

**Ideas**:
- Craft beer breweries

**Data Sources**: OpenStreetMap via Overpass Turbo

### Day 15 (Nov 15): Fire

**Description**: Energy, light, transformation: heat, wildfires, energy, night lights, volcanoes.

**Ideas**:
- Wildfire history
- Volcanic activity zones
- Night lights from space
- Power plant locations
- Heat map (literal temperature)

**Data Sources**: NASA FIRMS, NOAA, energy databases

### Day 16 (Nov 16): Cell
**Category**: Technical
**Description**: Discrete units/networks: raster cells, tessellation, cellular networks, disease spread.

**Ideas**:
- Hexagonal binning

**Data Sources**: Point data to aggregate, network data

## Week 3: Tools & Boundaries

### Day 17 (Nov 17): A new tool
**Category**: Challenge Classic
**Description**: Use software, language, library, or technique you've never used before.

**Ideas**:
- Try QGIS if you've only used web
- Python cartopy if you're JS-focused
- Blender for 3D terrain
- R's ggplot2 + sf
- Observable notebooks

**Data Sources**: Any, focus on learning the tool

---

### Day 18 (Nov 18): Out of this world
**Category**: Creative
**Description**: Non-Earth maps: fantasy realms, exoplanets, Moon, Mars, deep space, mental maps.

**Ideas**:
- Moon crater map
- Mars elevation
- Star map of constellations
- Fantasy world generator
- Exoplanet surface projection

**Data Sources**: NASA planetary data, astronomy catalogs, imagination

---

### Day 19 (Nov 19): Projections
**Category**: Technical (GIS Day)
**Description**: Focus on map projections. Unusual projections, highlight distortion.

**Ideas**:
- Projection comparison grid
- Tissot's indicatrix
- Dymaxion / Fuller projection
- Waterman butterfly
- Interrupted projections

**Data Sources**: Simple world boundaries, focus on projection

---

**Data Sources**: USGS, NOAA, HydroSHEDS
